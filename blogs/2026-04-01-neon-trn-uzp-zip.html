This page is a lookup-first reference for TRN, UZP, and ZIP. The goal is to make the lane movement easy to scan, especially when switching between mixed-source and same-source forms.

Mixed-source notation: vn = [a0 a1 a2 ...], vm = [b0 b1 b2 ...] Same-source notation: v0 = [x0 x1 x2 ...] The same-source case is the mixed-source case with both inputs drawn from the same x-sequence.

How to Use This Page

  • .16b
  • .8h
  • .4s
  • .2d

Start with .4s if you want intuition first. It is the easiest arrangement for seeing how TRN, UZP, and ZIP differ. Use the other sections as lookup tables once that pattern is clear.

At a Glance

TRN takes either the even lanes or the odd lanes, then interleaves them.

UZP takes either the even lanes or the odd lanes, then packs them toward the front.

ZIP takes either the low half or the high half, then interleaves it.

Six Patterns to Memorize

Instruction Mixed-source pattern One-line reading
trn1 [a0 b0 a2 b2 ...] Interleave even lanes.
trn2 [a1 b1 a3 b3 ...] Interleave odd lanes.
uzp1 [a0 a2 ... b0 b2 ...] Extract and pack even lanes.
uzp2 [a1 a3 ... b1 b3 ...] Extract and pack odd lanes.
zip1 [a0 b0 a1 b1 ...] Interleave the low half.
zip2 [aN/2 bN/2 ...] Interleave the high half.

The sections below expand those six patterns for each arrangement in the order .16b, .8h, .4s, and .2d.

Let
vn.8h = [a0 a1 a2 a3 a4 a5 a6 a7]
vm.8h = [b0 b1 b2 b3 b4 b5 b6 b7]

.4s

Let
vn.4s = [a0 a1 a2 a3]
vm.4s = [b0 b1 b2 b3]
v0.4s = [x0 x1 x2 x3].

Mixed-source

Instruction Result
trn1 [a0 b0 a2 b2]
trn2 [a1 b1 a3 b3]
uzp1 [a0 a2 b0 b2]
uzp2 [a1 a3 b1 b3]
zip1 [a0 b0 a1 b1]
zip2 [a2 b2 a3 b3]

Same-source

Instruction Result
trn1 [x0 x0 x2 x2]
trn2 [x1 x1 x3 x3]
uzp1 [x0 x2 x0 x2]
uzp2 [x1 x3 x1 x3]
zip1 [x0 x0 x1 x1]
zip2 [x2 x2 x3 x3]

This is the arrangement that usually gives the cleanest intuition for how the three instruction families differ.

.2d

Let
vn.2d = [a0 a1]
vm.2d = [b0 b1]
v0.2d = [x0 x1].

Mixed-source

Instruction Result
trn1 [a0 b0]
trn2 [a1 b1]
uzp1 [a0 b0]
uzp2 [a1 b1]
zip1 [a0 b0]
zip2 [a1 b1]

Same-source

Instruction Result
trn1 [x0 x0]
trn2 [x1 x1]
uzp1 [x0 x0]
uzp2 [x1 x1]
zip1 [x0 x0]
zip2 [x1 x1]

The key point for .2d: when there are only two lanes, trn1, uzp1, and zip1 all collapse to lane 0, while trn2, uzp2, and zip2 all collapse to lane 1. The arrangement flattens most of the differences away.

One-Line Summary

TRN = split by even / odd lanes, then interleave UZP = split by even / odd lanes, then extract and pack ZIP = split by low half / high half, then interleave
